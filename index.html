NHS Professional Registry

The primary goal of this page is to create a repository for the different types of professions available in the NHS. When you think of the NHS it's easily assumed that the only possibilities of work is in healthcare, but did you know that over 50% of NHS staff ahve no healthcare qualifcations? They, instead have found other work that suits them and the NHS as a whole better. Why not take a look at some of these availabilites?

Health Records Officers are responsible for managing patient health records. They make sure that they are accurate and kept up to date to be able to provide efficient health records service to patients.

They wear many hats in handling the financial, staffing, legal, clinical and clerical aspects of smooothly running an outpatient center.

As a biomedical scientist, you'll carry out a range of laboratory and scientific tests on tissue samples and fluids to help clinicians diagnose and treat diseases. You'll also evalutate the effectiveness of treatments.

As an administrator you will be responsible for liasing with medical staff, patients, carers, and visitors.You'll manage medical records, manage the running of receptions and nurse stations. You'll also be responsible for updating the clinic notes and lists.

You'll be responsible for providing a courteous recepetion service to patients and clinicians in line with office preocedures. You will also manage all calls presented to the Outpatient Appointment line relating to outpatient appointments.

Responsible for leading busy and dynamic teams. This includes the delivery of efficient and effective services in line with national and professional agendas. This will also invovle managing staff, monitoring performance, managing change and ensuring contractual targets are met.

You will focus on effective delivery of clinical and reception duties. These include the inputting and maintenance of accurate patient information on Trust information systems, and providing cross cover support to ward administrators.

You'll be responsible for overseeing administrative tasks in an office to ensure that they are performed adequately and in a timely manner. This also includes performing tasks such as filing, producing reports and ordering office supplies.

You will manage and monitor the Trust computing environment. This will include being responsible for routers, switches, security appliances, and wireless access points.

You will work closely with the medical and nursing teams, being responsible for the flow of patients within the day case ward. This involves the preparation of patient information, electronic updating of demographics and booking treatment for patients as requested by the clinican.

You'll provide professional medicines management advice and support to services to ensure compliance with legislative and regulatory requirements. This means delivering high-quality, safe, efficent, and cost effective medicines management systems and processes.
